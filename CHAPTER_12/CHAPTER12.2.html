Briard

by Ivy Duckett

The briard, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers becuase of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the Frist Word War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of briards is slowly recovering Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all ownd briards.
